What is CardioConnect?

CardioConnect is a connected set of tools designed to streamline how cardiology field teams schedule, coordinate, and track cases — replacing the patchwork of processes used across Watchman, EP, and CRM today.


It's an ecosystem of three components that work together as one connected system:

Console
The schedule is built
Go App
The work is executed
Dashboard
Metrics are tracked

Together, this creates one connected system for how your team plans, executes, and improves.

These tools are actively being developed. They are not final — and that's exactly why you're here. Your hands-on feedback this week will directly shape how these tools work when they roll out to the broader field.

How CardioConnect Works Differently

Before you dive in this week, there are a few concepts worth understanding. CardioConnect introduces a new way of managing cases — here's the mental model.

Work Orders Are Planned Placeholders
When you add a case in CardioConnect, you're creating a work order — a planned slot on the schedule. It doesn't need to have every detail yet. Think of it as "here's what's coming up."
Unassigned Is Normal
You can add a case without assigning it to anyone — that's by design. Unassigned work orders show up in the console so managers can assign them based on availability and coverage.
Work Order → Procedure Worksheet
After a case is completed, you'll create the procedure worksheet from the work order. The work order is the plan; the procedure worksheet is the record. They're two separate steps.
This Replaces Your Current Process
Instead of adding cases to personal calendars or shared spreadsheets, you'll add them here. Everyone works from the same system — one source of truth for your team's schedule.

Why You're Here

We're building CardioConnect with the field, not just for the field. Before these tools go live, we need to know they actually work for the people using them every day — and that's you.

Friday Retro

Let's come back together to reflect on the week and talk about what we learned.

What This Session Is About

This is your chance to share your honest experience with the CardioConnect tools. Remember — these tools are still being built. Nothing is set in stone. The whole point of this week was to put them in your hands so we can learn what's working and what needs to change before they go live.


There are no wrong answers. If something felt off, confusing, or unnecessary — that's exactly what we need to hear.

What Worked Well?

Think about moments this week where the tools felt intuitive, useful, or better than how you do things today.

  • What tasks were easy to complete?
  • What features felt most valuable to your day-to-day work?
  • Were there any moments where something just clicked?
  • What should we make sure to keep as-is?

What Was Frustrating or Confusing?

Think about moments where you got stuck, felt lost, or something didn't behave the way you expected.

  • Where did you struggle or need to guess?
  • What felt like extra work compared to your current process?
  • Did you run into any errors or unexpected behavior?
  • What felt missing or incomplete?

What Would You Change?

If you could redesign or improve anything about the experience, what would it be?

  • What would you add, remove, or do differently?
  • How would you change the workflow to better match how your team actually operates?
  • What would make this easier for your peers to pick up?

Rollout Readiness

Thinking about the broader field team and what's ahead:

  • On a scale of 1–10, how ready does this feel for a wider rollout?
  • What would need to be true for your team to adopt this confidently?
  • How would you describe CardioConnect to a colleague who hasn't seen it?
  • What training or support would people need?

Thank You

Your time and feedback this week are shaping the future of CardioConnect. What you shared helps us build something that actually works for the people who'll use it every day.
